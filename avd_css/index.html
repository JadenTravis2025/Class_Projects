NASA’s Artemis Program is a bold initiative to return humans to the Moon for the first time in over 50 years. Building on the legacy of Apollo, Artemis aims to land the first woman and the next man on the lunar surface, explore more of the Moon than ever before, and establish a sustainable human presence there. This effort will not only advance scientific discovery but also prepare us for the next giant leap — sending astronauts to Mars.

Mission Tasks

  • Launch the Orion spacecraft aboard the Space Launch System
  • Conduct crewed lunar flybys and landings
  • Build and operate the Lunar Gateway space station
  • Establish Artemis Base Camp on the Moon

The Journey Ahead

Artemis I successfully tested NASA’s new deep space systems without a crew, paving the way for Artemis II — the first crewed mission to orbit the Moon. Following that, Artemis III will land astronauts on the lunar surface, focusing on the Moon’s South Pole, a region rich in resources like water ice. These missions will demonstrate new technologies, strengthen international partnerships, and inspire the Artemis Generation to push the boundaries of exploration.
